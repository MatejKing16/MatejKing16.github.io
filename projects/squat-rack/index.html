End-of-Arm Tooling for Bag-in-Box Palletization

March 2026 · Graduation Thesis

Overview

Designed and engineered a complete End-of-Arm Tool (EOAT) for a FANUC R-1000iA/100F industrial robot to automate the palletization of Bag-in-Box (BiB) liquid packaging. The project involved vacuum system design, structural FEA analysis, technical documentation, and full BOM preparation for manufacturing. Successfully achieved a safety factor of 4.8 with a total EOAT weight of 1.7 kg while handling a 10 kg payload.

Technical Details

Structure & Materials: Base plate fabricated from EN-AW 5083 aluminum (8mm thickness) for lightweight yet robust construction. All structural components designed to withstand the dynamic stresses of a 6-axis robot operating at speeds up to 400 mm/s.

Vacuum System: SMC ZK2A15K5PL-06-B ejector provides the vacuum generation, with four SMC 50mm anti-slip suction cups providing a total contact area of 6,540 mm². Digital PNP proximity sensor with adjustable hysteresis enables automatic grip confirmation and fault detection.

Design & Analysis: Full SolidWorks CAD model with FEA analysis to verify structural integrity under operating loads. Technical drawings prepared according to ISO 13715 standards. Complete 18-component BOM with supplier information and cost analysis included.
